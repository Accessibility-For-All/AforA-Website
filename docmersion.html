FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about how DocMersion turns your documents into accessible web experiences.

How does DocMersion actually work?

DocMersion uses OCR, advanced image processing, and proprietary AI models to analyze your PDFs and rebuild them as clean, semantic HTML. It reconstructs headings, reading order, tables, and generates alt text for images automatically. Complex documents may also receive human review by accessibility specialists. All processing runs securely on U.S.-based AWS infrastructure.

What accessibility standards does DocMersion target?

DocMersion is built with the goal of producing accessible documents that align with major modern accessibility standards, including:

  • WCAG 2.1 AA — our primary accessibility target
  • ADA Title II and Title III guidelines (including the DOJ's 28 CFR Part 35 ruling)
  • Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act (36 CFR 1194.22)
  • EN 301 549 (EU accessibility requirements)
  • State-level guidance including California AB 434, the Unruh Act, and Colorado HB 21-1110

As standards and regulations evolve, DocMersion is actively maintained and updated. The platform is regularly audited by independent accessibility experts, automatically keeps documents accessible as they change, and aims to incorporate new requirements such as WCAG 2.2/3.0 as they are adopted.

Does the accessible HTML version contain the same content as the original document?

Yes. The accessible HTML version DocMersion produces is designed to be equivalent in content to the original document, which is central to its goal of producing genuinely accessible experiences aligned with WCAG 2.1 AA.

  • All text, headings, lists, tables, and key visual information are preserved. Charts and complex visuals are converted into data tables or detailed descriptions.
  • Structure, reading order, alt text, contrast, and keyboard navigation are all handled so assistive technology users can access all the same content programmatically.
  • DocMersion treats the HTML transcript as the primary accessible format, with the original PDF available for printing and archival.
Does using DocMersion mean we no longer need to manually remediate our PDFs?

Yes. Because DocMersion creates a fully accessible HTML transcript of your documents — PDFs and many other formats — that transcript can serve as an alternative accessible format, which means your original files do not require separate remediation.

What types of PDFs does DocMersion support? What about complex or messy documents?

DocMersion is specifically designed for messy and complex PDF documents. It uses OCR, advanced image processing, and proprietary AI models to interpret content, and complex pages get additional human review by accessibility specialists at no extra cost. There is no surcharge for difficult documents — the goal is to make seemingly unfixable files accessible and targeting WCAG 2.1 AA.

Does DocMersion work on scanned PDFs?

Yes. DocMersion handles scanned PDFs well. It uses OCR, advanced image processing, and AI models to turn scanned pages — including handwriting, maps, diagrams, budgets, and complex layouts — into searchable, screen-reader-friendly HTML. Complex or low-quality scans are explicitly supported, and there is no extra charge for them.

What if our documents don't have proper heading or tag structure?

DocMersion does not rely on your existing heading or tag structure. Instead it runs OCR and AI over the PDF, then rebuilds the structure automatically into clean, semantic HTML — headings, lists, tables, and correct reading order. The resulting transcript aims to be accessible and WCAG 2.1 AA-aligned for structure and reading order, even if the original PDF has no tags or incorrect ones.

Which file types are supported?

DocMersion supports a wide range of document formats — far more than just PDFs. See What types of documents does DocMersion handle? for the full list.

What types of documents does DocMersion handle?

We're proud that DocMersion works across a broad range of document formats, not just PDFs. Whatever your organization uses, DocMersion is built to handle it:

  • PDF — including scanned, complex, and untagged files
  • Microsoft Word (.doc, .docx)
  • Microsoft Excel (.xls, .xlsx)
  • Microsoft PowerPoint (.ppt, .pptx)
  • Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides
  • OpenDocument formats — Writer (.odt), Calc (.ods), and Impress (.odp)
  • Rich Text Format (.rtf)
  • Plain text (.txt)
  • CSV — spreadsheet and tabular data files
  • Common image formats — PNG, JPEG, WebP, and TIFF (including scanned documents and photographs of text)

In every case, DocMersion converts the source file into a clean, accessible HTML experience that is searchable, translatable, and screen-reader friendly — no matter how the original document was created.

Will DocMersion work on unpublished or intranet documents?

DocMersion currently works only with publicly accessible document links — PDFs and other supported formats that anyone can reach via a public URL. It cannot process documents on intranets, behind logins, or otherwise restricted from public access. The team is working on expanding to private and intranet environments, but that capability is not yet available.

Does DocMersion bypass intranet restrictions?

No. DocMersion can only activate on publicly accessible document links. If a PDF is on an intranet, behind a login, VPN, or any access control, DocMersion cannot retrieve or process it. It does not circumvent authentication, firewalls, or permissions in any way. Restricted documents remain restricted.

How does the accessible version work with screen readers?

Screen readers will announce the presence of an accessible version and the keyboard shortcuts available to navigate the DocMersion viewer. The accessible version can be toggled directly with Ctrl + Shift + V, along with additional shortcuts available through the hotkeys menu.

How does DocMersion handle fillable PDFs?

DocMersion provides an accessible HTML version for reading, navigating, translating, and understanding the form content. Users can still download or open the original PDF to complete and submit the form in their PDF viewer. This ensures the form content is accessible while preserving existing submission workflows.

Will enabling DocMersion slow down our site?

No. DocMersion is designed so it should not noticeably affect your site's load speed:

  • The helper script loads asynchronously, after your main page content, so it does not block page rendering.
  • It inventories links and enables documents in the background once your site has finished loading.
  • The document interface loads up to 10× faster even on slower connections or with unoptimized PDFs, and a Safe Mode option uses compressed images for very large documents.

If you ever notice slowness, you can temporarily disable DocMersion on a specific page or contact our support team to help diagnose and optimize performance.

Does DocMersion require ongoing document maintenance?

No additional remediation work is required on your end. Continue posting PDFs as usual. DocMersion automatically detects when a document at a given URL has changed and reprocesses it, keeping the accessible version current. Optional manual edits and fine-tuning are available but not required.

Does DocMersion support Single Sign-On (SSO)?

Yes. DocMersion supports Single Sign-On via a range of identity providers, including:

  • Microsoft / Azure AD (Entra ID)
  • Google
  • Facebook
  • Any other standards-based identity provider via SAML 2.0 or OpenID Connect (OIDC)
Can you explain the AI in more detail for a leadership or board briefing?

DocMersion uses AI in several focused ways to turn PDFs into accessible, interactive experiences without requiring manual remediation for most documents:

  • Accessible HTML transcript generation: AI interprets layout and structure (headings, lists, reading order), extracts text including from scanned PDFs via OCR, and produces accessible HTML output targeting WCAG 2.1 AA.
  • Image understanding and alt text: For figures, charts, maps, and other visuals, AI generates descriptive alternative text. Existing PDF alt text is incorporated where available.
  • Complexity detection and routing: AI flags pages likely to be high-complexity — dense tables, diagrams, low-quality scans, handwriting — so they receive additional handling, often including human accessibility specialist review at no extra cost.
  • Document Q&A: AI powers a feature that lets end users ask natural-language questions about a document and receive answers grounded in that document's content, working even with typos and in multiple languages.
  • Translation: AI supports on-the-fly translation of the accessible transcript into over 150 languages, with prioritization based on languages commonly spoken in the service area.
  • Change detection and reprocessing: When a PDF changes at the same URL, the system detects the update and automatically reprocesses the document so the transcript stays current.
What testing has DocMersion gone through?

DocMersion goes through extensive, ongoing testing to validate both accessibility and reliability. This includes automated accessibility scanning, manual code review for semantic structure and ARIA correctness, and real-world testing with assistive technologies such as screen readers across major platforms and devices.

The platform is also reviewed by independent accessibility consultants on a regular basis, and we maintain active feedback loops with users of assistive technology to catch and resolve real-world friction points. DocMersion receives frequent updates — both for new features and ongoing accessibility improvements — so the platform continues to improve over time.
